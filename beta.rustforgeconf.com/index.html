What is Rust Forge?

Rust Forge is a two part conference held over four days for those interested in building with the Rust programming language.

  • part 1: team spaces, community building, workshops
  • part 2: single-track conference with breakout spaces

Whatever your team’s journey with Rust is, you’ll find something to learn, something to share, and something to build upon. At Rust Forge, the hallway track is a major focus. There will be ample opportunities to meet community members, share ideas, and enjoy the beautiful surroundings of Wellington.

Where and When is Rust Forge?

Rust Forge will take place on August 27-30, 2025 at Shed6 in Wellington, New Zealand.

27–28:
Training + Coding
29–30:
Presentations + Conversations

Why New Zealand?

Wellington was selected for its beauty and accessibility. It features affordable and accessible amenities, a deep/rich culture, and undeniable natural beauty. It’s a perfect place to gather, learn, and forge the next big thing.

Further details for sponsors

Making Payments

Once you've been in contact, we'll generate an invoice from Accelerant Limited (the event's sponsoring organization) that's payable via these payment systems:

  • Australia - Direct Entry (BECS) in AUD from a business bank account
  • New Zealand - Direct credit in NZD
  • USA - ACH in USD from a business bank account
  • Worldwide - SWIFT (wire) transfers in USD

If you use one of the local payment options, we will convert the USD amount to the local currency and include the conversion rate in the body of the invoice.

Bring your family

Conduct and Safety

Everyone participating in the event and its online spaces agrees to the Rust Project’s Code of Conduct. While in New Zealand, you are also subject to New Zealand law.

Active incidents

In an emergency, dial 111 from any phone and ask to speak to the Police.

  • 111 is New Zealand's emergency number
  • The event venue's address as "Shed 6, Queens Wharf, Wellington"

In non-emergency situations, please use whichever channel you prefer:

  • Come to the reception desk
  • Speak to a security guard
  • Contact an event volunteer
  • Email <safety@rustforgeconf.com>
  • In the Discord server, tag members with the #safety role

Preventing incidents

If you would like to raise a possible safety concern before the event or offer advice to prevent breaches of the code of conduct:

  • Email <safety@rustforgeconf.com>

Retrospective Reports

To notify us of an issue after the event has finished, then please use one of these channels:

  • Email <safety@rustforgeconf.com>
  • Contact the Police via 105

Anonymous Reports

If you would like to raise a complaint avoid revealing your name to the event's organizers, then please:

  • Contact the Rust Project's moderation team <rust-mods@rust-lang.org>

Note: The moderation team accepts anonymous submissions, but may be limited in their response if you wish to remain anonymous.
